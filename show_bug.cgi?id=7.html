Description alvanx 2011-12-08 22:59:47 EET 
BibleTime 2.9 messes up Hebrew text (and presumably other RTL text?). It seems that the letters are force displayed as LTR. It is effectively turned into "siht ekil txet a" - you have to read it backwords to get it. 
This happens in the text display as well as the mag window.

Comment 3 alvanx 2011-12-23 13:39:43 EET 
I tried Esra SIL and SBL Hebrew, which are the most commonly used (and probably the best free) Hebrew fonts. Most others will have some kind of problem. I have used SBL Hebrew in BibleTime for years with no problem, so it's not the font. I tried OSMHB, WLC, Aleppo and HebModern. I can't read Arab or Syriac, so Hebrew is the only thing I can check. The problem occurs in parallel view as well as in single view. 

Here is what the text is supposed to look like (hope this website will display it right): בְּרֵאשִׁ֖ית בָּרָ֣א אֱלֹהִ֑ים

For double-check comparison, look at Gen 1:1 online: http://www.academic-bible.com/en/nc/online-bibles/hebrew-bible-bhs/bibelstelle/Gen%201,1/anzeige/context/#iv

Even if you don't know Hebrew, you can compare where you find the major characters (ignore the dots and dashes around (those are vowels), but they are what made me notice the bug in the first place). The behavior of the text is as follows: 

The words are in correct order. When you compare the two first words (on the right side), you will see it is bereshit (בְּרֵאשִׁ֖ית, "in the beginning"). What BibleTime displays is "tihsereb" (the equivalent of "ni eht gninnigeb"). 

Can you see the problem? There are minor variations with the other module, but they share the same basic issue.
Comment 4 alvanx 2011-12-23 22:57:33 EET 
One more specification: Hebrew modules that include vowels (WLCD and OSMHB) act as described regarding word order: They retain the word order. The first word of a sentence is on the right side, even though the first letter of the word is on the left. 

Modules without punctuation actually also twist the word order so that an RTL text is completely turned LTR. The first word of each sentence is on the left side, and the first letter of the word is on the left side as well.

Comment 7 Gary Holmlund 2011-12-24 03:49:10 EET 
I believe I have narrowed down this problem to a Qt bug. I am attaching a png file with Gen 1:1 shown from academic-bible.com and BibleTime on LinuxMint 12, Fedora 16, and openSuse 12.1. BibleTime was the latest git master. 

LinuxMint 12 correctly shows the text, but Fedora 16 and openSuse 12.1 do not. LinuxMint 12 is built from Ubuntu 11.10 so it should be correct also.

Here are the Qt versions of each.

Fedora 16
  qt 4.8.0
  qtwebkit 2.2.0

openSuse 12.1
  qt 4.7.4
  qtwebkit 2.2.0

linuxmint 12
  qt 4.7.4
  qtwebkit 2.2~2011week36-0ubuntu1
 
LinuxMint 12 and Ubuntu 11.10 took a prelease of webkit and named it "2.2~2011week36" and it is working. The others have the final 2.2.0 webkit and they are not working right. 

So, my conclusion is that after 2010week36 a bug was introduced into webkit 2.2. I have search the bug list for webkit but have not found a report like this problem. I will probably have to write a small program to demonstrate the problem and submit a bug report.
